Event: Locust swarms appear on map — follow them and defeat the Gnoster variant at the end
Triggers when Nightlord is: Gnoster
Invader spells / abilities:
Pest Thread Swarm of Flies Kindred of Rot's Exultation Aura Rotten Breath (Incantation)

🫧
Unifying Fate
Event: Giant Bubbles forming across the map — step inside bubbles, then defeat the lesser Augur that spawns
Triggers when Nightlord is: Gnoster, Caligo, or Heolstor
Invader spells / abilities:
Bubble Shower Thops's Barrier (Magic Shield) Crystal Barrage Carian Slicer
Buff: Flasks of Crimson Tears also restore FP in addition to HP on each use — effectively combines your two resource potions for the rest of the run.
🕷️
Favor of the Night
Event: Purple/dark orb appears in the sky — locate and defeat all Night Horde enemies (night swarms) in the area
Triggers when Nightlord is: Gnoster, Libra, or Harmonia (DLC)
Invader abilities:
Ensnaring Grasp (grab) Finger Slam AoE Madness Aura Curse Stacking
Buff: Ultimate Art Gauge fills significantly faster for the entire expedition. Lets you use Ultimate Arts in boss phases much more frequently — essentially cuts gauge charge time by ~40%.
👁️
Traces of Grace-Given Lord
Event: A Fell Omen appears on the map — defeat it before it reaches and kills players
Triggers when Nightlord is: Caligo, Fulghor, or Heolstor
Invader spells / abilities:
Omen Bairn (Holy explosion) Cursed-Blood Slice Fell Omen Shackle Scarlet Aeonia burst
Buff: Attack power increases each time you interact with a newly found Site of Grace. The bonus compounds — clearing more of the map pays off with more ATK stacks heading into the Night boss.
😈
Demon's Plating
Event: "A demon casts its curse" message — all players' max HP is halved. Pay Runes to lift the curse: ~15,000 (Night 1) / ~35,000 (Night 2). One player's payment covers the whole team.
Triggers when Nightlord is: Gladius, Maris, or Fulghor
Curse / passive traits:
Max HP Halved (curse) No combat required — pay or endure
Buff: After paying: all players receive Demon's Plating — a defensive shell reducing incoming damage for the entire expedition. Also counts as a free Wish (no item or boss requirement). One of the safest buffs — just bank Runes before Night.
💡 Tip: Stop leveling at character level 10–11 if you want max attack power scaling — ATK efficiency drops significantly past that point.
🐕
Tricephalos Pack Hunt (DLC)
Event: A pack of Tricephalos hounds invades across the map in three waves — wolf-dogs with coordinated flanking AI. Defeat all waves before they scatter or the timer expires.
Triggers when Nightlord is: Gladius (DLC variant)
Pack abilities:
Pack Howl (ATK aura) Frenzy Bite (bleed buildup) Split Lunge (3-wolf simultaneous charge) Coordinated Flank
Buff: Maximum Stamina permanently increased for the entire run (~+25% base Stamina). Also grants a Stamina regen boost after rolling — aggressive roll-catching and combo-extending playstyles benefit enormously. One of the best quality-of-life buffs heading into the Night Lord.
⚔️
Rise from Near-Death (DLC)
Event: 3 Balancers spawn at the camp and your team is teleported to face them. You must kill all 3 before the storm touches them — failure means you lose all Healing Flasks for the rest of the run.
Triggers when Nightlord is: Balancers / Harmonia (DLC only)
Balancer valkyrie spells / abilities:
Golden Vow (self-buff) Radahn's Spear Barrage Erdtree Barrier (block) Scarlet Aeonia Dive Wrath of Gold (AoE) Dual Greatsword Combo
Buff: Rare chance to automatically rise from near-death (works like Wending Grace / Bloodboon). Each time you rise, ATK power is permanently boosted for the run. Stacks if you trigger it multiple times. One of the strongest buffs in the game.

Physical ATK Up
+0: +4% · +1: +5% · +2: +6% · +3: +10.5% · +4: +12%
Five tiers labeled +0 through +4. Each is a fixed multiplier on physical damage: +0=+4%, +1=+5%, +2=+6%, +3=+10%5, +4=+12%. Multiple copies from different sources stack additively. Shown in-game as "Physical ATK Up +1" etc., not as Delicate/Polished/Grand.

Legendary Weapon Passives are unique effects tied to specific named weapons. Unlike generic weapon passives, each is unique — you cannot duplicate them. Critically, they vary in where the weapon must be for the passive to activate:
✔ Offhand + Sheathed ⚠ Offhand only (not sheathed) ✗ Held only (active weapon)
Two-handing: You can always two-hand any weapon (Triangle+R) regardless of which slot category it falls under. The category only describes where the weapon needs to be for its passive to activate — two-handing a weapon counts as "held" and triggers all passive categories.
Left hand slot: Placing a weapon in the off-hand (L1/L2 slot) without two-handing still triggers "Offhand" and "Offhand + Sheathed" passives.
